Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The man of igniting inspiration

Here's a time line of Dr. Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • 1954 - Graduated from St. Joseph College"
  • 1955 - Went to MIT to study engineering.
  • 1960 - Joined DRDO as Chief Scientist
  • 1965 - He started working on a rocket project
  • 1969 - Got Government's approval for his expandable project. He was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969 as the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1981- honored with a prestigious awards from the Government of India including the Padma Bhushan.
  • 1982-83 - Under the able guidance of Kalam, several missiles including Agni, an intermediate range ballistic missile and Prithvi, the tactical surface-to-surface missile, were developed
  • 2002 - Kalam stood for presidency in 2002 and was sworn in as the 11th president of the Republic of India on 25 July 2002.
  • 1990- honored with several prestigious awards from the Government of India including the Padma Vibhushan.
  • 2013 -recipient of the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society "to recognize excellence in the management and leadership of a space-related project".
  • 2015 - dies at the age of 76.

"Kalam's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress."
